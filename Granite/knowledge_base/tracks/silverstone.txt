Silverstone Circuit - Circuit Guide

CIRCUIT OVERVIEW

Silverstone is located in Northamptonshire, England, and has hosted the British Grand Prix since 1950. The current 5.891 km, 18-corner layout was reconfigured in 2010 with a new Arena infield section. The circuit is regarded as one of the finest high-speed challenges in motorsport, featuring long flowing sections and the legendary Maggotts-Becketts-Chapel complex. Silverstone has two DRS zones on the Wellington Straight and the Hangar Straight.

CAR SETUP

Silverstone is a high downforce circuit due to the numerous fast corners that require aerodynamic grip. The Maggotts-Becketts complex in particular demands strong lateral stability. Wind plays a major role at Silverstone and can change the handling balance significantly between laps. Rear tyre wear is the primary concern due to the high lateral loads through the fast corners.

TURN 1 — ABBEY

A flat-out right-hander taken in sixth gear in a modern F1 car. There is no need to brake. Position the car on the left of the track and begin turning just after the 100-metre board. Take as much inside kerb as possible without cutting the corner. The orange sausage kerbs are not large enough to significantly affect stability. Run wide to the left on exit which sets up the approach to Farm.

TURN 2 — FARM

A flat-out left-hand curve following Abbey. Keep steering smooth to avoid scrubbing tyres. The main objective is to stay tight to the left on exit to open up the entry for Village. Any running wide here compromises the entry to the first heavy braking zone.
