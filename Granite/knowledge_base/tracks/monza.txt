Autodromo Nazionale Monza - Circuit Guide

CIRCUIT OVERVIEW

Monza is the fastest circuit on the Formula 1 calendar, located inside the royal park of Monza north of Milan. The 5.793 km circuit was built in 1922 and has hosted the Italian Grand Prix at every World Championship season except 1980. Known as the Temple of Speed, it produces the highest average speeds of any F1 circuit and demands the lowest downforce configuration of the season. F1 engines are at full throttle for nearly 80 percent of the lap. There are only six corner complexes: the first two chicanes, the two Lesmos, the Ascari complex, and the Parabolica.

CAR SETUP

Monza demands one of the smallest wing angles on the F1 calendar to minimise drag on the long straights. A low downforce setup maximises top speed but can make the car feel unstable through the faster corners. Brake bias should be set slightly towards the front to manage the heavy braking zones at the chicanes. Tyre wear is relatively low compared to other circuits due to the lack of high-load corners.

TURN 1-2 — VARIANTE DEL RETTIFILO (FIRST CHICANE)

Cars approach Turn 1 at 340 km/h in eighth gear and brake approximately 120 metres before the chicane, entering at 86 km/h in second gear. Stay to the left-hand side on approach and begin braking just before the 150-metre board. Brake hard and keep the car in a straight line, using aggressive downshifts to maximise engine braking.

Turn in late and aim for a late apex just wide of the orange sausage kerb. Avoid the sausage kerb on the inside as it will launch the car and cost significant lap time. After the first apex, apply a small burst of throttle before rolling off and dabbing the brake to make the second apex. Keep as far left as possible to open up the second apex.

Good traction out of Turn 1 is critical for lap time as it feeds directly onto the long Curva Grande. A small mistake here costs time across the entire next section.

TURN 3 — CURVA GRANDE

A long, sweeping right-hander taken at near full throttle in seventh gear after the first chicane. Focus on keeping the car stable and positioning it to the right to set up for the entry to the Variante della Roggia. Any correction or lift here affects speed all the way down to the next chicane.

TURN 4-5 — VARIANTE DELLA ROGGIA (SECOND CHICANE)

Brake point is just after the 150-metre board and the orange block on the barrier. Brake hard and downshift to second gear. Aim for a late apex and avoid the sausage kerbs as they unsettle the car and can send it towards the gravel trap. Use all the track on corner exit and run the wheels along the top edge of the kerb for maximum lap time. If the throttle has to be rolled off completely for Turn 5, too much speed was carried through Turn 4.

TURN 6 — CURVA DI LESMO 1

A medium-speed blind right-hander entered at 273 km/h in sixth gear, apexing at 217 km/h. There is a slight banking through the corner which can be used to advantage. Apply a short dab of the brakes and downshift to fifth gear. Turn in and clip the apex cleanly. Avoid riding too much of the inside kerb as it unsettles the rear end. Use all the track on exit but avoid running too wide onto the gravel.

TURN 7 — CURVA DI LESMO 2

Seventh gear entry at 278 km/h, apexing in fifth gear at 203 km/h. Lesmo 2 is slightly tighter than Lesmo 1 and has less runoff on exit. Requires careful balance. Brake precisely and stay tight to the apex on turn-in. It is very important to use all the kerb on exit. A mistake at either Lesmo results in a spin into the gravel, while good exits set up an overtaking opportunity into Variante Ascari.

TURN 8-10 — VARIANTE ASCARI

A fast left-right-left chicane named after Alberto Ascari. The downhill approach beneath the bridge features an uneven surface. Brake late but maintain control through the sequence. The goal is to carry as much speed as possible while keeping the car stable. The section is regarded as one of the more technically demanding sections of the lap and important for achieving competitive lap time.

TURN 11 — CURVA PARABOLICA (ALBORETO)

Cars approach at 335 km/h in eighth gear. Brake at approximately 50 metres and begin to rotate the car in towards the apex smoothly. Downshift to sixth gear as the car slows. The Parabolica is a constant radius corner — a quick gentle turn-in, a long consistent apex, and a gentle exit.

Avoid the inside kerb as it can cause significant instability. As soon as the apex kerb is reached, accelerate hard while keeping steering smooth and as open as possible. The exit speed from the Parabolica directly determines top speed on the main straight, which in turn affects overtaking opportunities into Turn 1. Every tenth of a second lost at the Parabolica costs lap time all the way down the straight.

TYRE STRATEGY

Tyre wear is low at Monza due to the minimal lateral loading through corners. A one-stop strategy is typically the best option. Starting on medium compound and switching to hard for the final stint is the standard approach. The low tyre wear means tyre pressures can be set higher than at most circuits.

DRS AND OVERTAKING

Monza has two DRS zones: on the main start-finish straight and on the back straight. The combination of long straights and heavy braking zones at the chicanes makes Monza one of the best overtaking circuits on the calendar. Slipstreaming is a key tactic on the main straight, particularly into Turn 1.
