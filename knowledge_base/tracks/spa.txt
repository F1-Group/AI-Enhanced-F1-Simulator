La Source (Turn 1) is a tight hairpin at the top of the hill immediately after the start. It is a common overtaking spot on the first lap. The key is a late braking point and early apex to maximise acceleration down the hill towards Eau Rouge.

Eau Rouge and Raidillon is the most iconic corner complex in Formula 1. The car goes downhill through Eau Rouge and immediately uphill through Raidillon at high speed. Modern F1 cars take this flat out. Any lift here is a significant time loss. The key is to keep the car as straight as possible through the compression at the bottom.

The Kemmel Straight follows Raidillon and is one of the longest straights in Formula 1. DRS here makes overtaking at Les Combes very common.

Les Combes (Turn 5) is a fast chicane at the end of the Kemmel Straight. Heavy braking from high speed is required. A good exit from the second part of the chicane sets up the run through Malmedy.

Pouhon (Turn 9) is a fast double-apex left-hander taken at high speed. It requires full commitment and smooth steering. Understeer here is caused by too much entry speed.

Blanchimont (Turn 18) is taken flat out and leads into the Bus Stop chicane. Confidence here is critical as any lift costs significant time on the following straight.

The Bus Stop chicane (Turns 18-19) is the final corner before the start-finish straight. A smooth exit is important for a good lap time.

Spa is a high energy circuit with a mix of high-speed and technical sections. Tyre warm-up is critical in cold or wet conditions. The weather at Spa can change very rapidly, sometimes raining at one part of the circuit while dry at another. Always monitor weather data carefully.

Rear tyre wear is a concern through the high-speed corners. Managing energy deployment through the fast sections helps preserve rear grip.
