Copse (Turn 1) is a high-speed right-hander taken flat out in modern F1 cars. Any hesitation or lift here costs significant time and confidence through the following complex.

Maggotts, Becketts, and Chapel (Turns 3-7) is the most iconic section of Silverstone. This high-speed left-right-left sequence requires maximum commitment and precise steering inputs. The key is to maintain rhythm through the complex without upsetting the car balance. Understeer through Becketts is a common issue caused by too much front load.

Stowe (Turn 9) is a high-speed right-hander at the end of the Hangar Straight. Braking is done in a straight line before turning in. Overshoot here leads to a very slow exit onto the Vale straight.

Club Corner (Turn 13) is the final corner before the start-finish straight. A late apex is critical to maximise acceleration onto the straight. This corner is heavily influenced by wind direction.

Silverstone is a high downforce circuit with high lateral loads through the fast corners. Rear tyre wear is the primary concern, especially through the Maggotts-Becketts complex. Managing rear degradation is key to a strong race pace.

The circuit rewards smooth steering inputs. Aggressive corrections through the high-speed sections cause unnecessary tyre wear and slow the car down.

Wind plays a major role at Silverstone. A headwind on the Hangar Straight reduces top speed significantly. Tailwinds can make Copse feel very different lap to lap.
