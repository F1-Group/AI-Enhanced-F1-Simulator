Monza Turn 1 (Variante del Rettifilo) requires heavy braking from 340 km/h down to 80 km/h. The braking point is at the 150 metre board. Late braking causes understeer and a wide exit onto Curva Grande.

Curva Grande is taken flat out at high speed. Any lift here costs significant time on the following straight.

The Lesmos (Turn 5 and Turn 6) are medium speed corners taken in sequence. The key is a clean entry to Turn 5 to set up a good exit from Turn 6 onto the back straight.

Variante Ascari (Turn 8-10) is a fast chicane complex. Smooth inputs are critical here. Avoid mounting the kerbs aggressively as it unsettles the car.

The Parabolica (Turn 11) is the final corner before the main straight. A late apex is essential to maximise exit speed onto the straight. Every tenth of a second lost here directly costs top speed on the following straight.

Monza is a low downforce circuit. Tyre wear is generally low due to the lack of high-load corners. Soft tyres can run longer than at most circuits. Fuel saving is possible on the long straights by lifting and coasting early.

DRS is available on the main straight and the back straight, making overtaking relatively common at Monza compared to other circuits.
